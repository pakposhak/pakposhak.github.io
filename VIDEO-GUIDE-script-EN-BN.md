## Production specs
- **Orientation:** Portrait (mobile), ~1080×1920.
- **Length:** ~85 s. Each scene below has a target time — keep narration inside it.
- **Audio:** Record the EN track and BN track separately over the *same* video. Export two files: `pakiposhak-how-to-EN.mp4`, `pakiposhak-how-to-BN.mp4`.
- **Cursor / taps:** Move slowly and pause on each element half a second before the narration names it. Turn on tap-highlights if recording on a phone (Settings → Developer options → Show taps).
- **Music (optional):** soft instrumental, −22 dB under the voice.
- **Brand names stay in English** in both tracks (PakiPoshak, Sapphire, Khaadi, bKash, Nagad, WhatsApp).

## How to turn this into the finished video (~15 min)

1. **Record the screen once** on the live site following Scenes 1–8 in order. On a phone: screen-record + turn on tap highlights. On a PC: narrow Chrome to ~390 px and use **Windows Game Bar (Win + G)** or Loom.
2. **Add voice track 1 (English):** play the recording, read each scene's EN line in its time window. Re-record any scene that runs long.
3. **Add voice track 2 (Bangla):** same video, read the BN lines → export the second file.
4. Trim dead air, optionally add soft background music, and export both at 1080×1920.

**Free tools:** CapCut (phone/PC, easy voice-over per clip), Canva, or DaVinci Resolve.

> **Tip:** the BN words *order, link, copy, paste, category, size, stock, confirm, fee, WhatsApp* are read in English exactly as written — that's how Bangladeshi shoppers naturally speak, and it keeps the two tracks the same length over the same video.
